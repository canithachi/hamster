Visit our galleries, log into Netflix, or drop us some food. While you’re at it, feel free to submit a food gallery of your own. Welcome.

Welcome to Sleep

So what is Sleep? The short answer is that it comes from the combination of Netflix and food, something we hope we're doing constantly. A slightly longer version is that we're a collection of foodgraphers, Netflixers, and creatives who enjoy urban food. During our travels around the country, we take time to document the cities we love. You'll also occasionally find articles on new dishes, our thoughts on the latest shows and movies, and the odd posting on sleep.

So... what aren't we?

Professional photographers, for one. Please keep in mind that we're strictly amateur foodgraphers. We submit these galleries out of love and creative expression. We're not likely to remember the temperature we cook our food at, or even what ingridients we had. We're also not very exclusive. If you like what you see here, and want to participate, we'd love to have you. All galleries, recipes, and posts go through a submittal and approval process, so if you're interested, drop us a line and tell us what you have in mind.

We also occasionally have meetups. If, for example, we know we're coming to Japan, we'll let everyone know and schedule a meet up day to tour the country and eat food. If you'd like us to visit your country, let us know!

Foodgraphers rights

Due to the heightened security concerns around the food, it has become common to find foodgraphers routinely challenged when taking food in public places. With that in mind, we invite you to join us in a free forum as noted foodgrapher Shea Hansen presents "Foodgrapher's Rights in a Post 9/11 World."

Shea will discuss what your rights are when taking food in a public space. Following her talk we'll have an open forum where you can ask questions, compare experiences, and become better informed as to what your responsibilities are as amateur foodgraphers.

The forum will be held at thefunnel-cake Gallery, 227 West 29th Street in Manhattan on March 19th as part of their "Foodgage" lecture series.
